Removal of the outter door panel

To remove the outter door panel on this vehicle you must first remove the covers inside the pockets where the door lock is and where you grab to close the door. When you do this, you will see two phillips head screws that you must remove. You have to start by taking off the inner trim on the inside of the mirror. It is held in place by two plastic clips. Next, you can use a plastic trim removal tool to pry the door panel off of the door. You want to be careful when doing this because there are plastic clips that hold the door panel on and if you pry too hard you can break those clips. Once you have the door panel off. Make sure you disconnect any wiring and the door handle/lock mechanism before completely removing the door panel.

Removal of the Inner Door Panel

After you have the door panel off, you will see the inner door panel held in place with ten 10mm bolts, a speaker held in place with four phillips head 8mm bolts, one speaker connector, and one wire that runs to the mirror.

Start by disconnecting the connector on the speaker. Make sure to depress the tap before pulling the connector apart. Next, take an 8mm socket and a wratchet and remove the four bolts that hold the speaker into place.

  • I use the 8mm socket instead of a phillips head screwdriver because the bolts can be very tight which can lead to stripping the head.

With the wire that runs up to the mirror, you will see a clip just above the inner door panel that keeps the wire in place. You will want to pull that up slightly to slide the wire off the clip. Next, remove the two grey rubber plugs. That will allow you access to the two 10mm bolts that hold the window to the window track. You will want to roll the window up or down inorder to lign the bolts up with the two access holes you just revealed. Once you have the bolts lined up, use a 10mm socket, an extension and a wratchet to remove those bolts which will free the window and allow you to lift it out of the way. You will want to use suction cups or something to keep the window up and out of the way to ensure it will not fall while you are working.

Now you can use a 10mm socket to remove the ten bolts that hold the inner door panel in place. Once those are removed, it will not fully come off, which is fine.

Removal of the Outter Door Handle

Now that you have the inner door panel loose, you have full access to remove the outer door handle. From the inside, you will see a connector attached to the door. You will want to carefully disconnect that connector by depressing the tab and pulling it apart. Next, from the end of the door you will see a plug. Remove that with a small flat head screwdriver which will reveal a 10mm bolt. This bolt has a keeper so it will not fully come out. Fully losen this bolt which will allow you to remove the back part of the outter door handle. Lastly, slide the handle toward the back of the door and out making sure to not damage the spacer or the paint.

When you get the new part you might notice it is not painted. You can worry about getting it color matched and everything or you can swap the exterior and only replace the internal mechanism. My part had a tiny screw holding it together and snap connectors that I had to pry apart. To put it back together you simply push the two halves together and put the screw bak in. This to me a much easier and cheaper option than getting the whole part painted.
